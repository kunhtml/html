Question:

Sketch a creep curve for a typical metal. Ensure that you label the axes correctly, and indicate the different regions of creep behaviour on the graph.

Creep:

A deformation that is time-dependent and observed at constant stress value and elevated temperatures is known as creep and failure due to this condition is known as creep failure

Answer and Explanation: 1

The figure below represents the creep curve for a typical metal.

Creep curve

In the creep curve of a metal the initial deformation of the metal is elastic till point A after which the region of primary creep starts where the deformation rate decreases with time till point B.


In secondary creep region the deformation rate is almost constant and the creep curve is almost linear from points B to C.


In the tertiary creep region the rate of deformation increases rapidly till the fracture point is reached where the metal gets fractured.
